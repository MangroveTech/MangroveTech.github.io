  • Welcome to Wave

    Posted on Dec 2014, 2014

    Everyday we rely more on our smartphones. For browsing the web, for playing games, and for of course for communicating. It has become natural for us to hold conversations by trading short, quick messages back and forth over instant messaging.

    And yet, email is still slow and complicated on our phones. Mobile email apps are only shrunk down versions of desktop programs. And on a small screen all those extra features get in the way of what is important, your messages and your connections.

    With Wave we’ve decided to rethink email from the beginning with an app designed for mobile from the phone up.

    This is not our first shot at making an email app. In 2013 we launched Seed Mail, an all-in-one mail client that aimed to recreate the desktop Outlook experience. Well received by enterprise users, for consumers we felt like it strayed too far from our core vision: “redefine email for the mobile era.”

    Wave is a result of this refocus, an app specifically designed for the mobile experience.

    There are a number of problems with the inbox on our phones. Messages are too long, and replies often come too late. Subject lines and signatures take up valuable space on a small screen. And messages from actual people get lost in a sea of newsletters from robots.

    We’ve created an app that makes email relevant again by combining the strength of both instant messaging and email. Instant, real-time conversations, all backed up and searchable through your inbox. And anyone with an email address can be involved in the conversation.

    Predating even the Internet, it’s easy to forget just how old email is. Despite rumors of its demise, email’s robustness and universality has kept it at the core of how we communicate. But we get lost in email because it has been used for everything, from scheduling meetings to sharing files.

    With Wave we’ve brought email back to what it does best—send simple, quick messages to people.
